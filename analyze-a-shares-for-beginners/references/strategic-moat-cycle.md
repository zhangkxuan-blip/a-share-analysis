# 国家战略、行业壁垒与产业周期

## 国家战略相关性

Do not answer whether a stock itself is a national core until separating the policy target from the listed company. Use the current national five-year plan, approved special plans, implementation rules, budgets, procurement lists, standards, and company operating evidence.

Grade the chain:

- P0 unrelated: no formal policy link.
- P1 broad theme: a general direction mentions the sector, but no task, target, budget, or implementation route exists.
- P2 explicit industry task: a formal plan names the product, technology, capacity, penetration, security, or localization objective.
- P3 implementation: a competent authority publishes targets, projects, procurement, subsidies, quotas, standards, or a dated implementation mechanism.
- P4 company exposure: the company has verified qualifications, orders, projects, revenue exposure, capacity, or standards participation that connects it to P2 or P3.

Use national strategic core only when P2 or P3 is strong and P4 is supported. Report policy grade, document number, publication and effective dates, 2026-2030 horizon, beneficiary mechanism, company exposure percentage when disclosed, implementation lag, and reversal risk.

Primary starting points include the Fifteenth Five-Year Plan outline approved in March 2026, State Council policy database, NDRC, MIIT, sector competent authorities, and provincial plans. Prefer the national outline and formal special plans over broker theme labels.

## Industry Barrier Card

Score each supported dimension from 0 to 5 and show the evidence date:

1. technology, patents, know-how, yield, or process control;
2. qualification, regulation, certification, or customer verification;
3. capital intensity, capacity scale, cost curve, and learning effects;
4. resources, licenses, locations, or exclusive supply;
5. channel, installed base, ecosystem, data, or network effects;
6. switching cost, service continuity, and customer lock-in;
7. brand, price premium, and repeat purchase;
8. management execution and R&D conversion.

Do not total the score when more than 30% of the dimensions lack evidence. Distinguish durable barriers from temporary shortage, policy protection, and high cycle prices.

## Leadership, Substitutability, Scarcity, And Premium

Classify the company as national leader, segment leader, regional or niche leader, follower, or unverified. Compare market share, revenue or shipment rank, capacity, gross margin, ROIC, R&D, standards participation, customer quality, and channel coverage.

Evaluate substitutability from qualified alternatives, customer switching time, verification cycles, supply security, compatibility, service network, and price sensitivity. Label irreplaceability high only when alternative supply is limited and switching is costly and slow; never infer it from market-share rank alone.

Evaluate scarcity from resource uniqueness, license scarcity, process yield, patents, customer certification, and capacity lead time. Evaluate premium from disclosed average selling price, gross-margin premium versus comparable peers, renewal or retention, and customer willingness to pay. A high share without margin or return premium may reflect scale rather than scarcity.

## Domestic Substitution And China-US Exposure

Build a product-level dependency matrix:

| Layer | Required evidence | Output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| upstream materials and components | supplier geography, annual report, procurement disclosure | domestic, imported, mixed, unknown |
| equipment and software | key equipment, EDA or industrial software, license and service dependence | controllability and bottleneck |
| company product | performance, yield, certification, mass production, orders | concept, candidate, verified replacement |
| downstream customers | verification cycle, domestic share, customer concentration | adoption evidence |
| trade controls | official Chinese and foreign control lists | restriction and compliance risk |

Classify domestic substitution as concept only, verification stage, small-scale shipment, mass-production replacement, or internationally competitive. Require product performance and commercialization evidence; policy language alone is insufficient.

Separate import dependence, export dependence, and foreign-customer dependence. Use company filings for company-specific ratios and customs data only for industry context. When a company does not disclose supplier geography or product origin, mark unknown rather than estimating.

## Industry Cycle And Duration

Trace demand -> orders or shipments -> inventory -> price -> utilization -> margin -> capex -> new supply. Use at least three independent indicators and two time windows.

Classify stage as contraction acceleration, contraction slowing, early recovery, expansion acceleration, late expansion, or downturn confirmation. State the observed start window and an evidence-based duration range; do not claim an exact end date.

Estimate duration from the sector's historical order, inventory, capacity, and capex cycle. Compare at least two prior cycles where data exists. Structural growth can coexist with a cyclical downturn.

## Cyclical, Growth, Or Hybrid

- Cyclical: earnings mainly follow commodity prices, capacity, spreads, utilization, or inventory.
- Growth: earnings mainly follow penetration, market expansion, share gains, repeat demand, and scalable unit economics.
- Hybrid: structural penetration grows but capacity and inventory still drive large earnings cycles.

Evaluate industry ceiling with addressable market, penetration headroom, replacement frequency, price trend, policy constraint, resource constraint, international market, and return on incremental capital. Do not use a single TAM estimate without source and date.

## Latest Products And R&D

Review products launched, approved, shipped, or materially upgraded in the latest 12 months. Separate announcement, prototype, customer verification, small-batch shipment, mass production, and recognized revenue.

Show R&D expense, R&D intensity, capitalized development share, R&D staff, major projects, patents or standards when material, and commercialization evidence. Compare three-year trend and peers. Treat high R&D as investment only when technical and commercial milestones progress; otherwise raise efficiency and impairment risk.

## Component-Level Import Dependence Matrix

Separate technology autonomy from procurement and revenue geography. For each decisive component or material, record supplier category, known supplier country/region, import-share disclosure, domestic alternatives, switching cost, and evidence date. Also separate revenue export share. If supplier geography or import share is not disclosed, write unknown; do not infer zero import dependence from domestic intellectual property.

## Order Overlap And Product Commercialization

Treat order backlog, new awards, signed contracts, framework agreements, and bid announcements as potentially overlapping populations. Never sum them unless the company explicitly reconciles the non-overlap. Show announcement gross value, known net incremental value, execution period, cancellation conditions, and revenue-recognition uncertainty.

For a “latest star product,” show stage separately: announcement, prototype/verification, small-batch shipment, mass production, and recognized revenue. R&D spending alone is not evidence of commercialization.
